Comparing the top 7 best free keyword research tools of 2026 includes 1. Google Keyword Planner, 2. Google Search Console, 3. Google Trends, 4. Ahrefs Webmaster Tools, 5. Ubersuggest, 6. AnswerThePublic, 7. Keywords Everywhere.

Picking the wrong tool costs more than a subscription fee: it shapes what keywords you target, which content you create, and ultimately how much organic traffic you earn. This list evaluates tools on two criteria: the depth of data available on the free tier and whether the limitations are disclosed upfront rather than buried in upgrade prompts.

### 1. Google Keyword Planner

Best for: PPC advertisers and SEO beginners who want free, Google-sourced keyword volume data and bid estimates.

[Google Keyword Planner](https://ads.google.com/home/tools/keyword-planner/) is a free keyword research tool built into Google Ads. It is maintained by Google and draws directly from the same dataset that powers Google's ad auction system. The tool is positioned primarily for advertisers planning paid campaigns, but it is widely used by organic SEO teams who want volume data from the source.

**At a Glance**

| | |
| --- | --- |
| Platform | Web app (within Google Ads account) |
| Free Plan | Yes: completely free; requires a Google Ads account (no active spend needed) |
| Starting Price | [Free](https://ads.google.com/home/tools/keyword-planner/) |
| G2 Rating | [Not confirmed: unmanaged profile (~10 reviews)](https://www.g2.com/products/keyword-planner/reviews) |
| Capterra Rating | Not listed on Capterra |

**What it does well:** Google Keyword Planner gives you keyword data that comes directly from Google's own search infrastructure. There is no middleman index, no estimation lag, and no third-party crawl to introduce inaccuracies. For advertisers mapping out campaigns, the combination of volume ranges, historical 12-month trend data, and bid estimates in a single interface is hard to replicate at any price.

- Discover keyword ideas seeded from a keyword phrase or a URL
- Search volume ranges with 12-month historical trend data
- Suggested bid estimates for Google Ads campaign planning

**Standout feature:** Location targeting down to city level is available at no cost, which makes it particularly useful for SaaS companies targeting geographic verticals or local service businesses assessing regional demand before committing ad budget.

**Limitation:** The free tier's most significant constraint is volume precision. Without active ad spend, the tool shows broad ranges such as 10K to 100K rather than exact monthly figures. For organic SEO work where prioritisation depends on precise volume comparisons between competing keywords, those ranges can make it difficult to rank opportunities confidently.

- Exact search volumes are hidden unless you run active ad spend
- Competition metrics reflect paid ad competition, not organic SEO difficulty

**Who it's for:** PPC teams building campaign keyword lists and SEO beginners who need credible volume data without paying for a third-party subscription.

**Who it's NOT for:** Organic SEO teams who need exact monthly search volumes or keyword difficulty scores to prioritise content investments.

**Editor's read:** We recommend this as the default starting point for any SEO beginner; the data quality is unmatched at the price of zero, even if the volume ranges frustrate advanced practitioners.

**Pricing**

Google Keyword Planner has no paid tier and no subscription fee. It is part of the Google Ads platform, which operates on an advertising spend model rather than a software licence. There is no cost to access the tool itself, as of June 2026. See the [Google Ads pricing page](https://ads.google.com/home/tools/keyword-planner/) for context.

| Plan | Price | Key Inclusions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Free (via Google Ads) | $0 | Keyword ideas, search volume ranges, historical metrics, bid estimates, location targeting to city level, language filtering |

**What Users Say**

**Love:** "Direct from Google's own data: search volume and bid estimates you can trust for paid campaigns." via [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/keyword-planner/reviews).
**Concern:** "Without active ad spend, you only see volume ranges (e.g. 10K–100K), not exact monthly figures." via [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/keyword-planner/reviews).

### 2. Google Search Console

Best for: Website owners who need authoritative, first-party data on how their own site performs in Google Search.

[Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console/about) is a free web tool from Google that shows you how your verified properties perform in Google Search. It covers everything from which queries drive clicks and impressions to Core Web Vitals scores and indexing errors. For understanding what keywords your site already ranks for, there is no more reliable source.

**At a Glance**

| | |
| --- | --- |
| Platform | Web app |
| Free Plan | Yes: completely free with no usage limits |
| Starting Price | [Free](https://search.google.com/search-console/about) |
| G2 Rating | [4.7/5 (497 reviews)](https://www.g2.com/products/google-search-console/reviews) |
| Capterra Rating | 4.8/5 (217 reviews) |

**What it does well:** Search Console delivers performance data that no third-party tool can replicate because it comes directly from Google's index. The Performance report breaks down clicks, impressions, CTR, and average ranking position by query, page, country, and device. That granularity is genuinely useful for identifying quick-win keyword opportunities where you rank on page two with strong impressions but low CTR.

- Performance report showing clicks, impressions, CTR, and average ranking position by query
- URL Inspection tool for checking crawl, index, and rendering status
- Core Web Vitals and page experience monitoring

**Standout feature:** The Coverage report flags indexing errors and excluded pages with specific error codes, which makes it the fastest way to find and fix pages Google is refusing to index. For [technical SEO](/saas-seo-agency/technical-seo-agency/) work, this is the diagnostic layer that paid tools approximate but do not replace.

**Limitation:** Search Console only shows data for your own verified properties. If you need to research competitor keyword strategies or identify untapped keyword opportunities beyond your current rankings, you will need an additional tool alongside it.

- Data is delayed up to 2 to 3 days; not suitable for real-time monitoring
- 16-month maximum data retention; no long-term historical export beyond that window

**Who it's for:** Any website owner or SEO professional who needs authoritative data on their own site's organic search performance, indexing health, and Core Web Vitals.

**Who it's NOT for:** Teams who need competitive keyword research, historical data beyond 16 months, or real-time ranking updates.

**Editor's read:** If you only install one tool from this list, make it Search Console; the data quality and depth are unmatched for understanding your own site, and the cost is zero.

**Pricing**

Google Search Console is completely free with no paid tier and no usage caps. It requires verifying ownership of the property you want to monitor. As of June 2026, there is no subscription model. See the [Search Console page](https://search.google.com/search-console/about) for setup instructions.

| Plan | Price | Key Inclusions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Free | $0 | Search performance data (queries, impressions, clicks, CTR, position), URL inspection, sitemap submission, Core Web Vitals monitoring, index coverage reporting, structured data enhancements |

**What Users Say**

**Love:** "A direct line to Google: I can see exactly what queries people used to find my site." via [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/210297/Google-Search-Console/reviews/).
**Concern:** "Data updates are delayed by 2–3 days and historical data retention is limited to 16 months." via [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/google-search-console/reviews).

### 3. Google Trends

Best for: Content strategists and marketers who need to spot seasonal trends, validate content timing, and compare topic momentum.

[Google Trends](https://trends.google.com/trends/) is a free Google tool that shows the relative search interest for any topic over time, broken down by geography and compared across up to five terms simultaneously. No account is required to use it. It is designed for trend discovery and topic comparison rather than precise volume measurement.

**At a Glance**

| | |
| --- | --- |
| Platform | Web app |
| Free Plan | Yes: completely free, no account required |
| Starting Price | [Free](https://trends.google.com/trends/) |
| G2 Rating | [4.6/5 (265 reviews)](https://www.g2.com/products/google-trends/reviews) |
| Capterra Rating | 4.7/5 (32 reviews) |

**What it does well:** Google Trends excels at answering timing questions. Whether a topic is growing, shrinking, or seasonal is immediately visible from the trend line. For content teams planning editorial calendars or deciding whether to prioritise a topic now versus in three months, the relative interest scores over custom date ranges are genuinely useful signals.

- Relative search interest trend lines over custom date ranges
- Geographic breakdown of interest by country, region, or city
- Compare up to 5 search terms or topics side by side

**Standout feature:** The "Related queries" and "Breakout" sections surface rising search terms before they reach peak volume. For [SaaS SEO](/saas-seo-agency/) teams looking to publish ahead of a trend rather than chasing it, this is the most useful free signal available anywhere.

**Limitation:** Google Trends reports relative interest on a 0 to 100 scale, not absolute monthly search volumes. You cannot size a niche or compare two keywords on the same absolute scale. For anything beyond directional trend validation, you will need a volume-focused tool alongside it.

- Shows relative interest scores (0 to 100), not absolute monthly search volumes
- Data for niche or low-volume topics is sparse and unreliable

**Who it's for:** Content strategists and marketing teams who need to validate timing decisions, spot emerging topics, and compare topic trajectories before investing in content production.

**Who it's NOT for:** SEO teams who need absolute search volume figures to prioritise keyword lists or size market opportunities.

**Editor's read:** We use Trends specifically for timing validation and spotting breakout terms; treat it as a directional signal layer, not a volume measurement tool.

**Pricing**

Google Trends is completely free with no paid tier, no account requirement, and no usage limits. As of June 2026, the tool remains openly accessible at [trends.google.com](https://trends.google.com/trends/).

| Plan | Price | Key Inclusions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Free | $0 | Relative search interest over time, geographic breakdown, related queries, trending searches, topic comparison across up to 5 terms |

**What Users Say**

**Love:** "Really intuitive: interesting insights into trending topics in just a few clicks." via [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/238898/Google-Trends/).
**Concern:** "Only shows relative popularity, not absolute search volumes: hard to size a niche precisely." via [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/google-trends/reviews).

### 4. Ahrefs Webmaster Tools

Best for: Site owners who want professional-grade backlink and technical SEO auditing for their own verified domains at no cost.

Ahrefs is an established SEO platform with a large backlink index and a strong reputation among professional SEO practitioners.

The Site Audit produces a Health Score and flags specific issue categories with clear explanations, which makes it useful for identifying quick technical wins without needing to read documentation.

- Site Explorer shows organic keywords and backlinks for your verified domain
- Site Audit scans for 170-plus technical and on-page SEO issues with a Health Score
- Web Analytics provides privacy-focused traffic data without cookies

**Standout feature:** The Site Audit crawl with 5,000 credits per month is more thorough than what most free tools offer. **Who it's for:** Site owners who need professional backlink monitoring and technical SEO auditing for their own domains and can work within the credit constraints of the free tier.

**Editor's read:** The best free technical SEO audit tool available; the credit cap on Site Explorer is a genuine constraint, but the audit capabilities alone justify the signup.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best free keyword research tool overall?

Google Keyword Planner is the strongest free option for volume data because it draws directly from Google's own search infrastructure, with no estimation layer. Pair it with Google Search Console for first-party performance data on your existing rankings.

### Does Google Keyword Planner show exact search volumes for free?

No. Without active Google Ads spend, Google Keyword Planner shows broad volume ranges such as 1K to 10K rather than exact monthly figures. Running even a minimal active campaign will unlock exact volumes.

### What is the difference between Google Keyword Planner and tools like Ubersuggest?

Google Keyword Planner provides Google-sourced volume and bid data for free but lacks organic difficulty scores. Ubersuggest adds a keyword difficulty score and competitor data at the cost of a 3-searches-per-day free limit.

### Which free tool is best for content ideation and question-based research?

AnswerThePublic is the most purpose-built option for question-based keyword discovery. It organises queries by question type, preposition, and comparison automatically. For teams managing larger keyword clusters, see [7 Best Keyword Clustering Tools for SaaS in 2026](/list/best-keyword-clustering-tools/).

### Is Keywords Everywhere actually free?

No. The extension installs for free but requires purchased credits to display keyword data. It is the only tool on this list with no genuinely free tier. If you need inline SERP keyword data at no cost, Google Search Console's Performance report is the closest alternative for your own site.

### Can I use these tools together?

Yes, and most SEO teams do. A common workflow combines Google Keyword Planner for initial volume discovery, Google Search Console for ranking gap analysis on your own site, Google Trends for timing validation, and Ahrefs Webmaster Tools for backlink and technical health monitoring. Each tool covers a distinct part of the research process.

### Which tools are best for a SaaS company specifically?

For SaaS keyword research, the combination of Google Search Console (own-site data), Ahrefs Webmaster Tools (backlink and technical audit), and Google Keyword Planner (volume discovery) covers the core workflow at zero cost.
