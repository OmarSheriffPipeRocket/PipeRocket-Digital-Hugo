## How I Evaluated These SaaS Marketing Agencies

30% – **SaaS Marketing Expertise**. How well the agency understands SaaS motions (PLG, freemium-to-paid, trial conversion, expansion), and whether they can speak fluently about MRR, ARR, CAC payback, and NRR. If the agency can’t talk about your business model, they can’t market it.

25% **– Pipeline and Revenue Attribution**. Whether the agency connects marketing spend to SQLs, pipeline, and closed-won MRR, not just traffic, leads, or cost-per-lead. This is where most agencies quietly fail, and I weighted it heavily.

20% – **Proven Results**. Documented SaaS pipeline outcomes, CAC improvements, and MRR growth from published case studies and verified client reviews on Clutch and G2.

15% – **Transparency and Reporting**. Whether you get live dashboards, clear answers on what’s being spent and why, and reporting framed in business language rather than platform metrics.

10% – **Pricing and Value**. Retainer structure, contract flexibility, and how clearly ROI is communicated relative to the investment. This matters most at Series A and Series B budgets where every dollar counts.

## Red Flags I Would Watch For When You Choose a SaaS Marketing Agency



**They report on MQLs and traffic as primary metrics.** In SaaS, MQL volume and sessions are activity metrics, not business outcomes. If an agency opens your reporting call with MQLs before discussing pipeline contribution or closed-won MRR, they’re measuring the wrong thing, and that misalignment will compound over the lifetime of your engagement.



**They can’t speak fluently about your SaaS metrics.** If an agency can’t have a natural conversation with you about CAC payback, NRR, gross margin, and LTV:CAC ratios, they don’t understand the business model they’re marketing for. SaaS marketing decisions that ignore unit economics produce growth that destroys margin.



**No SaaS-specific case studies with closed revenue outcomes.** Any agency can show you traffic graphs. Ask specifically for a SaaS case study where their work contributed to measurable pipeline or closed-won MRR. Verify the client is real and the metrics are business-level, not platform-level.



**They don’t ask about your sales motion before building campaigns.** SaaS marketing without understanding your sales process is just lead generation. Unqualified leads are worse than no leads because they consume your sales team’s time without moving ARR.



**They promise rankings or lead volume with no ICP conversation first.** SaaS marketing is ICP-led or it isn’t SaaS marketing. Any agency committing to traffic or lead targets without first understanding who actually buys from you is selling volume, not pipeline. Volume without fit is a tax on your sales team.

## Frequently Asked Questions About SaaS Marketing Agencies



### What does a SaaS marketing agency do differently from a general marketing agency?



SaaS marketing requires a fundamentally different approach than general marketing. Your revenue is recurring rather than transactional. Your buyers evaluate over weeks or months. Your CAC payback is measured against LTV, and your expansion revenue matters as much as new logos. A specialist SaaS marketing agency understands product-led growth, trial-to-paid conversion, ABM for enterprise motions, and how to attribute marketing spend to MRR, not just leads. General agencies optimise for conversions. SaaS marketing agencies optimise for pipeline, MRR, and CAC efficiency.



### Which channels work best for SaaS marketing in 2026?



SEO remains the highest-LTV channel for most SaaS. Content that ranks for comparison, alternative, and use-case queries compounds over time and produces pipeline at a fraction of paid CAC. LinkedIn is the highest-quality paid channel for reaching B2B SaaS decision-makers, particularly for deals above $20K ACV. Google paid search captures your existing category demand. Content and community build category authority that shortens your sales cycles. The right mix depends on your ACV, sales motion, and ICP, not on what the agency happens to specialise in.



### How do I know if my SaaS marketing agency is actually driving pipeline?



Ask your agency, in a single number, what their work contributed to pipeline and closed-won MRR last quarter. If the answer is expressed in sessions, MQLs, or cost-per-lead rather than SQLs, pipeline, or closed revenue, the attribution model is incomplete. A good SaaS marketing agency should be able to show you a report that traces marketing spend through to opportunity creation and closed-won MRR in your CRM, not just a channel-level activity report.



### How much should my SaaS company spend on marketing?



A common benchmark for B2B SaaS at Series A and beyond is 15% to 25% of target ARR allocated to sales and marketing combined, with marketing typically representing 40% to 60% of that. What matters more than the percentage is whether you have a clear model for what a qualified pipeline opportunity costs you, what your conversion rates look like from MQL to closed-won, and what your CAC payback target is. Spending without those three numbers produces growth you can’t defend to your board.



### Should I hire a full-service agency or specialist agencies per channel?



It depends on your ARR stage and internal team maturity. If you’re early-stage with no in-house marketing leader, a full-service partner or fractional CMO model that owns strategy and execution together will serve you better. If you’re Series B+ with a VP of Marketing and an internal team, you’ll get more leverage from specialist agencies in each channel (deep SEO, deep paid, deep content) coordinated by your in-house team. I see the failure mode in both directions. Early-stage teams that try to coordinate three specialists drown in management overhead. Late-stage teams that hire one full-service agency end up with mediocre work in every channel.
