## Frequently Asked Questions

### What should I look for in an HR tech marketing agency?

Look for agencies with genuine HR tech domain knowledge, not generalist B2B agencies that list HR tech as a vertical. The best HR tech agencies understand HRIS, ATS, HCM, and employee engagement platforms, know the HR buyer’s decision process, and can demonstrate pipeline attribution across their programmes rather than surface-level metrics.

### How much does an HR tech marketing agency cost?

HR tech marketing agency retainers typically range from $3,000 to $20,000 per month depending on scope and seniority. Boutique content-focused agencies start around $2,000 to $3,000 per month. Full-service demand generation agencies with pipeline attribution infrastructure typically start at $5,000 to $8,000 per month.

### What is the difference between an HR tech marketing agency and a general B2B marketing agency?

An HR tech specialist agency understands the HR buyer’s decision process, the compliance context surrounding HR software purchases, and the multi-stakeholder buying committees that involve HR, IT, finance, and legal. General B2B agencies require extensive onboarding to reach the same level of domain knowledge, which slows time to execution.

### Do HR tech marketing agencies work with early-stage companies?

Yes. Several agencies on this list, including PipeRocket Digital and Kalungi, are specifically designed for pre-revenue and early-stage HR tech founders. Kalungi provides fractional CMO leadership for pre-Series A companies. PipeRocket works with companies from zero ARR through Series B+, building the demand infrastructure from the ground up.

### How long does it take to see results from an HR tech marketing agency?

Paid media programmes typically show pipeline impact within 60 to 90 days. SEO and content programmes typically take three to six months to show measurable organic traffic and lead quality improvements. Full-funnel demand generation programmes, including attribution infrastructure, typically require a full quarter to build before reporting meaningful pipeline contribution.
