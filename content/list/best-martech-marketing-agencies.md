## Frequently Asked Questions

### What makes martech companies different to market to compared to other B2B SaaS categories?

Martech buyers are marketers themselves, which means they evaluate every vendor tactic critically. They are quick to identify generic content, distrust claims not backed by data, and rely heavily on peer recommendations and community opinions before engaging with a vendor. Agencies that understand this dynamic build programmes that earn trust through expertise rather than volume.

### How much does a martech marketing agency cost?

Martech marketing agency retainers typically range from $3,000 to $20,000 per month depending on scope, channel coverage, and seniority. Performance marketing agencies with pipeline attribution infrastructure typically start at $5,000 per month. Fractional CMO models with full execution, like Kalungi, start at $8,000 per month.

### What channels work best for marketing martech companies?

Paid LinkedIn and Google work well for direct pipeline from in-market buyers. SEO and thought leadership content builds long-term category authority. Community and dark social, including Slack groups, LinkedIn communities, and peer forums, are where martech buying decisions often form before a vendor is contacted. The best programmes cover all three in a connected system.

### Should a martech company use a specialist or generalist agency?

A specialist agency that understands how marketing technology buyers evaluate software will outperform a generalist in both content quality and campaign calibration. Martech buyers dismiss shallow content immediately, which makes domain expertise in the agency a non-negotiable requirement rather than a nice-to-have for companies competing in established categories.

### How long does it take to see results from a martech marketing agency?

Paid media programmes typically show pipeline contribution within 60 to 90 days. SEO and content programmes take three to six months to produce measurable organic pipeline. Demand creation programmes, including community influence and thought leadership, produce compounding results over six to twelve months as brand authority accumulates in the channels where martech buyers actually form opinions.
