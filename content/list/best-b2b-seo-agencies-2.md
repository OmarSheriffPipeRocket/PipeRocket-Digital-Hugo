## Why You Should go for “B2B” Specific SEO Agencies

**A word of caution:** Avoid the temptation to hire a generalist agency. Generalist agencies are built around consumer and non-B2B industries - ecommerce, retail, lifestyle brands. B2B is an add-on for them, not their core.

In B2B, the buying journey involves multiple stakeholders, long research phases, and technical evaluations. Generalist agencies often don’t account for this and:

- They apply the same SEO frameworks they’d use for an ecommerce store to your B2B software
- They treat a “lead” as a form fill, not a specific job title at a specific company size
- They celebrate traffic wins while your sales team drowns in unqualified leads - students, freelancers, businesses that can’t afford your product

I’ve seen this play out too many times. You need a B2B SEO Agency. Someone who:

- Knows what CAC, MQL, and SQL actually mean
- Understands that B2B revenue cycles are long and complex
- Builds strategy around your pipeline, not your pageviews

Think of it like medicine. For complex surgery, you want a specialist, not a general practitioner.

## How Would I Actually Choose from the Best B2B SEO Agencies?

So, once you have your shortlist, how do you pick? I’d suggest looking past the case studies for a moment.

### 1. Ask how they would bring revenue

Try not to ask “how will you rank me for X keyword?” Instead, ask “How does your strategy impact my pipeline velocity?”. If they hesitate, or if they go back to talking about “Domain Authority,” that’s a red flag. You want to hear about intent and solving pain points.

### 2. Ask the specific strategies they go for

If the pitch deck looks like it could apply to any company, be careful. Every B2B company is unique. If they haven’t done a deep dive into your competitors and your specific audience *before* asking for the business, they might be treating you like just another account. If their [B2B SaaS SEO checklist](https://piperocket.digital/blogs/saas-seo-checklist) is not malleable to your industry, then take that concern to them.

### 3. Ask how they handle when things go wrong

When you check references, try asking, “What happened when things went wrong?” SEO is volatile. Things change. You want a partner who calls you *before* you notice the drop and says, “Here is what happened, and here is the plan.” That is what a real partnership feels like.
