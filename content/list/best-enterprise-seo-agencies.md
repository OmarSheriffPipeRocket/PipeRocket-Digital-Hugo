## When Should You Hire an Enterprise SEO Agency?

Here are some questions you need to answer before thinking about hiring an enterprise SEO agency.:

### 1. Is your website too large for your internal team to manage?

When your site crosses tens of thousands of pages, internal teams hit a ceiling fast. An enterprise SEO agency brings the infrastructure and specialist teams to manage that scale without your internal team dropping everything else. Signs you’ve hit this point:

- Crawl issues are multiplying faster than they’re being fixed
- Index bloat is affecting ranking quality across large page categories
- Technical debt is older than your current SEO hire
- Site migrations are being delayed because nobody owns the SEO risk

### 2. Are you losing rankings to competitors despite having better content?

Better content doesn’t always win. If competitors are outranking you with weaker pages, the gap is rarely about content quality. An enterprise SEO agency diagnoses exactly where you’re losing. It’s usually one of these:

- Domain authority that hasn’t kept pace with competitors
- Crawlability issues preventing your best pages from being properly indexed
- Internal linking structures that dilute page authority across too many URLs
- Site architecture decisions made years ago that are silently killing performance

### 3. Are SEO fixes dying in your engineering backlog?

This is the most common enterprise SEO problem nobody talks about. Your team identifies the issue. It sits in a dev ticket for six months. Nothing ships. Enterprise SEO agencies are built to navigate this:

- They prioritize fixes by revenue impact, not SEO severity scores
- They translate technical requirements into language that moves engineering teams
- They identify workarounds that don’t require dev involvement at all
- They build implementation accountability into the engagement from day one

### 4. Are your marketing channels operating in silos?

If your SEO, paid media, and content teams are running separate strategies with no shared data, you are leaving serious revenue on the table. An enterprise SEO agency connects these channels. What that looks like in practice:

- Keyword intent data informing paid media targeting and bid strategy
- Content calendars built around search demand, not internal opinions
- Organic and paid coverage coordinated across the full buying journey
- Shared attribution models so every channel speaks the same revenue language

### 5. Are you expanding into new markets or product lines?

New market entries are where SEO debt compounds fastest. Poor architecture decisions made at launch take years to undo. Getting an enterprise SEO agency involved early prevents the most expensive mistakes:

- Site structure mapped to new buyer segments before any pages go live
- Keyword strategies built around how new markets actually search, not assumptions
- Authority signals established before competitors entrench themselves in the category
- Hreflang, subdomain, and URL structure decisions made correctly from the start

## How to Choose the Right Enterprise SEO Agency?

I’ve been on both sides of the table. I know that the questions you *usually* ask (like “What is your link strategy?”) are useless. Take it from an enterprise SEO agency ourselves, here is what you should actually be asking if you want to avoid a disaster.

### 1. Define the Goals You Want the Agency to Achieve

I see this mistake constantly. A marketing VP logs in and says, “I want more traffic.” Stop. Traffic is vanity. If you start with volume, you have already lost.

You need to chase intent. I focus my energy on “Bottom of Funnel” solution pages first. Why? Because I want the prospect who is actively searching for a vendor right now, not a student writing a research paper.

As an enterprise SEO service provider, I don’t want to hear “brand awareness” or “[lead generation](https://piperocket.digital/blogs/b2b-lead-generation/)” as the sole goal. Tell me you want to capture the person actively comparing you to your competitors. If your goal isn’t revenue, you are just burning the budget on server costs.

#### Here’s how one of our enterprise client discover calls went:

**The problem:**

The client had the usual pain points. They can’t focus on SEO and wanted someone to take over with authority. They told us the goal would be to increase traffic from a particular category.

**The issue:**

Bringing in traffic is not that hard. But there the issue with this is that, traffic is a vanity metric. We can bring in 30k traffic per month with 0 leads.

**How we aligned focus:**

We asked them their long term goal with us and SEO. Their replied that they want to make SEO a revenue engine. So, we asked them these things:

- What is your highest revenue making category?
- What are the channels that bring in traffic, SQLs, and conversions for this category.
- What is your current running SEO strategy for this category?

**The Result:**

With these information, we narrowed down to focusing on bringing in traffic and SQLs to this specific category initially and moved to other categories later. As of now, we’ve helped them get $5 Million in revenue from SEO using this strategy.

### 2. Evaluate the Enterprise SEO Agency’s Expertise

Most enterprise SEO agencies guess. They run a generic crawl, hand you a massive spreadsheet of errors, and walk away. That doesn’t work in enterprise. I need to understand the product better than the people who built it.

**Ask them this:** “How do you handle internal roadblocks?” If they don’t have a plan for getting tickets through a frozen engineering backlog, run. 

You don’t need a checklist; you need an enterprise SEO company who understands how to navigate compliance, legacy code, and dev teams that hate SEO. If they don’t understand the *friction* of your organization, they will never get anything shipped.

### 3. Review their Case Studies

Ignore the “200% traffic increase” slides. That is often just “fluff traffic” from a viral blog post that drove zero revenue. Look for the “fixer” stories.

If I was looking for an enterprise SEO agency, I would want to see where they took a site with millions of pages of “zombie content,” pruned it, and improved lead quality. I want to see how they handled a failed migration that tanked rankings.

That is the only case study that matters: Efficiency at scale. If they can’t show you how they turned organic search into a predictable revenue engine, they aren’t ready for enterprise complexity.

### 4. Assess the Tools and Resources they Use

You have to know if they are guessing, or forecasting. I don’t guess growth; I model it.

Your enterprise SEO agency should be looking at Total Addressable Market (TAM) within search. They should be analyzing your log files to see how Google actually crawls your site, not just what a third-party tool says.

If their strategy is just “write more blog posts” without analyzing crawl budgets, index bloat, or strike-distance keywords, they are gambling with your timeline. They need to show you the math on *why* a technical fix will result in revenue.

### 5. Consider their Reporting and Analytics Monitoring Practices

Most SEO reports are useless. They show “Average Position” or “Impressions”.

I would demand to see business impact. I would like to know which specific landing pages are driving SQLs (Sales Qualified Leads). If they aren’t setting up proper attribution to tell you that “Page A” generated “$50k in Pipeline,” they are hiding behind vanity metrics.

You need to know if you are ranking for terms that actually close deals, or just terms that get clicks. Demand to see a dashboard that speaks the language of your CFO, not your webmaster.

### 6. Ask for Referrals

Don’t ask for their happiest client. Ask them about the client with the messy tech stack.

Ask them about a time they had to fight a dev team to get a canonical tag fixed. In enterprise, things break, and politics get in the way, I’ve seen this myself and it happens more than you think. So, you need to know if they have the experience to push back when a “site update” threatens to wipe out your traffic.

You want a referral that confirms the agency acts like an owner, not just a vendor who sends a monthly PDF.

### 7. Consider Your Budget

Clients often ask, “How much does SEO cost?”. That is the wrong question. The question is: “What is the cost of *inaction*?”

If your site has technical debt preventing you from ranking for high-value terms, you are bleeding money every day. Calculate the LTV of a customer. If a monthly contract is worth $10,000 and you get two conversions every month that adds up to more than $10,000, you’ll be making more than the investment.

When you view it this way, you stop buying “deliverables” and start buying market share.
