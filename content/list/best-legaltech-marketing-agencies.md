## FAQs

### What makes legaltech marketing different from standard B2B SaaS marketing?

Legal buyers are trained skeptics who distrust vendor content. Effective legaltech marketing requires earned-media credibility and buyer-psychology-aware messaging, not just SEO and PPC.

### How much does a legaltech marketing agency typically cost?

Entry-level retainers start around $3,999/mo (Bay Leaf, Concurate). Growth-stage engagements run $5,000-$15,000/mo. Enterprise programmes start at $25,000+/mo.

### Should a legaltech SaaS company use a legaltech-specialist agency or a broad B2B SaaS shop?

Depends on your buyer. If you sell to attorneys and legal ops, a vertical specialist handles messaging nuance better. If you sell to CLOs at enterprises, a full-funnel B2B SaaS agency may deliver more pipeline faster.

### How long does legaltech SEO take to show pipeline results?

Most SEO programmes need 4-6 months for pipeline contribution. Technical fixes can appear in weeks. Content-led authority programs compound over 12-18 months.

### Does legaltech marketing require PR alongside SEO?

For products sold to law firm partners and CLOs, yes. Attorneys rely on trade press and peer referrals. Law360 and American Lawyer placements carry credibility that paid search alone can't buy.

### What channels work best for legaltech demand generation?

SEO for research-phase buyers, paid search for in-market buyers, LinkedIn ABM for enterprise legal ops, and PR for brand credibility with attorney decision-makers. The best legaltech programmes run at least three of these in parallel.

### What should I ask a legaltech marketing agency before hiring them?

Ask for named legaltech client references, how they define and report pipeline attribution, what their content approval process looks like for legal-sensitive topics, and whether they've run campaigns targeting law firm partners versus in-house legal teams.
