## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best affordable B2B PPC agency for SaaS companies?

### How much does B2B PPC management cost?

B2B PPC management costs vary based on pricing model, ad spend, and scope. Flat-fee retainers typically range from $1,000 to $10,000 per month. Percentage-of-spend models charge 10% to 25% of monthly ad budget. Hybrid models combine a base retainer with a percentage fee. Entry-level agencies like Getuplead start at $1,400/mo, mid-market agencies like KlientBoost and JumpFly start at $1,000 to $1,500/mo, and premium agencies like Directive start at $6,500/mo. Most agencies charge separate setup fees ($500 to $2,000) and don't markup ad spend, though some do.

### What's the difference between affordable and cheap B2B PPC agencies?

Affordable B2B PPC agencies deliver pipeline value at accessible pricing, transparent reporting, and honest scoping. Cheap agencies optimize for cost per lead without visibility into what happens after the click, report on platform metrics instead of pipeline, and often use junior staff or offshore teams. Affordable agencies like PipeRocket, Getuplead, and KlientBoost start at $1,000 to $3,000/mo but tie campaigns to SQLs, pipeline, and revenue. Cheap agencies may quote $500/mo but deliver junk leads that sales won't touch. The cost isn't the price, it's the wasted ad spend.

### Should I hire a B2B PPC agency or build an in-house team?

Hire a B2B PPC agency if you're under $10M ARR, don't have a dedicated performance marketer, or need expertise across multiple channels (Google, LinkedIn, Meta, Reddit) without hiring three specialists. Build in-house if you're over $20M ARR with consistent ad budgets exceeding $50K/mo and need full-time ownership of campaign strategy, creative, and attribution. The break-even point is usually around $15M ARR. Below that, an agency delivers faster results, broader channel expertise, and lower fixed costs than hiring, onboarding, and managing internal PPC talent.

### How do I know if a B2B PPC agency is actually affordable?

A B2B PPC agency is affordable if the retainer cost plus ad spend delivers positive ROI within 90 days, measured in pipeline created and closed revenue, not just leads. Calculate your acceptable CAC (customer acquisition cost) and LTV (lifetime value). If the agency's monthly retainer is $3,000, your ad spend is $10,000, and you close $50,000 in revenue from that pipeline within 6 months, the agency is affordable. If you're spending $5,000/mo on retainer and ad spend but generating zero pipeline, the agency is expensive at any price. Affordability is pipeline contribution divided by total cost, not the retainer number alone.

### What channels should an affordable B2B PPC agency manage?

An affordable B2B PPC agency should manage Google Ads (search and display), LinkedIn Ads (for B2B targeting), and at minimum one additional channel (Meta, Reddit, Microsoft Ads, or YouTube) based on where your ICP is active. Single-channel agencies (Google-only) limit your reach and create dependency. Multi-channel agencies spread budget too thin if they don't prioritize based on ICP behavior. The best affordable agencies start with Google and LinkedIn, then expand to Meta or Reddit based on performance data, not platform defaults. PipeRocket, KlientBoost, and Getuplead all manage 3+ channels under one retainer without charging separately per platform.
