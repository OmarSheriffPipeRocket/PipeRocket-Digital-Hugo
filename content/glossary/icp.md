## Why ICP matters more than persona

ICP is where most SaaS marketing programmes succeed or fail. Persona work - the marketing director, the head of revops, the VP of demand gen - gives you message tone. ICP gives you the right *companies* to send those messages to in the first place. If your ICP is wrong, persona-perfect copy lands in the wrong inbox.

## What an ICP definition includes

- **Firmographics** - industry, headcount, ARR band, geography
- **Tech stack signals** - current tools that suggest the buying readiness for yours
- **Buying triggers** - funding rounds, hiring patterns, exec changes
- **Disqualifiers** - companies that look like ICP on paper but never close (e.g. wrong stage, wrong region, wrong stack)

## How to write your ICP

1. Pull every closed-won account from the last 18 months. Filter for accounts that retained 12+ months and expanded.
2. Look for the firmographic patterns common across that set.
3. Layer in the tech stack and triggers that preceded the deal.
4. Write down the disqualifiers - accounts that signed but churned, or accounts your sales team rejected.

The output is a one-page document everyone in marketing, sales, and product can reference. If your ICP runs longer than a page, it's probably wrong.

## Common ICP mistakes

- **Treating ICP as aspirational, not actual.** ICP describes who buys today, not who you wish would buy.
- **Confusing ICP with TAM.** Total addressable market is the universe; ICP is the slice of it you can actually win.
- **Updating ICP only once a year.** ICP shifts as your product matures. Revisit it every two quarters.
