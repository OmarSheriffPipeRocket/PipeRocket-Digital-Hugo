## Why MQLs are widely misused

Most B2B SaaS dashboards treat MQLs as if they were pipeline. They aren't. An MQL is a **score** - a threshold a contact crossed inside a marketing-automation tool, usually triggered by content downloads, page visits, or webinar signups. Scoring models are downstream of marketing's worldview and rarely match the way sales teams actually qualify accounts.

## What we use instead

For most B2B SaaS programs we run, MQLs become a **leading indicator**, not a primary metric. The metrics that move pipeline conversations:

- Pipeline created (in $)
- Qualified opportunity rate (SQO conversion)
- CAC payback period
- Revenue attributed by source

## When MQLs still matter

Top-of-funnel diagnostics - sudden MQL drops can flag a content or paid issue before it shows up in pipeline. Just don't pay sales bonuses on them.
