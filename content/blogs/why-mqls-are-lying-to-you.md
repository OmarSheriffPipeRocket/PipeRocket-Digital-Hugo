## The MQL theatre problem

Most B2B SaaS dashboards still report [MQLs](/glossary/mql/) as if they were pipeline. They aren't. An MQL is a *score*, not a *commitment*. When marketing optimises toward a score, sales gets a long list of contacts who clicked something - not a list of accounts ready to talk.

> "If a metric doesn't move pipeline or revenue, we don't optimise toward it."

We learned this the hard way running [performance marketing](/glossary/what-is-performance-marketing/) inside SaaS companies before we built PipeRocket.

## What we track instead

The four numbers we actually surface to a CEO or board:

1. **Pipeline created** - by source, segment, and campaign
2. **CAC payback** - by [ICP](/glossary/what-is-icp/) fit
3. **SQO conversion** - qualified-opportunity rate, not lead-to-MQL
4. **Revenue attributed** - multi-touch, not last-click

### Pipeline created

This is the dollar value of opportunities created in the period. Filter by ICP fit so you don't reward volume that the sales team will reject.

### CAC payback

How long before each acquired customer pays back the cost to acquire them. If it climbs over 18 months for SaaS, you're scaling the wrong channel.

## How to make the swap

If you're switching off MQLs as a primary metric:

- Get pipeline-stage definitions agreed with sales **first**. No swap survives the first marketing report if sales disagrees on what "qualified" means.
- Wire up source attribution **before** you change the dashboard. You'll need a clean baseline.
- Sunset MQL goals in the next quarterly plan, not mid-quarter.

## When MQLs still matter

MQLs aren't useless - they're just not a *primary* metric. They're a **leading indicator** for the top of the funnel and a useful debugging tool when pipeline drops. Just don't pay bonuses on them.

## Wrap

Marketing leaders who survive the next two years will be the ones who stopped reporting on what was easy to measure and started reporting on what their CFO actually cares about. The board doesn't ask about MQLs. They ask about pipeline, payback, and revenue. Track that.
