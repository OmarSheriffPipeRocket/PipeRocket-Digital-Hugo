Most SaaS companies we talk to know they need saas link building but have no real strategy behind it. They publish blog posts, hope other sites link to them, and wonder why their competitors are outranking them despite similar content. The problem is rarely effort. It is that link building for SaaS works differently than the generic playbooks suggest, and the tactics that matter most are the ones most teams skip entirely. This guide covers the saas link building strategies that actually compound into rankings, organic traffic, and pipeline — and it starts with a mistake almost every team makes from day one.

- SaaS link building is the practice of acquiring [backlinks](/glossary/what-is-a-backlink/) to build search authority specifically for a software company’s most important pages
- Most saas [backlinks](/glossary/what-is-a-backlink/) go to blog posts when product, comparison, and pricing pages are what actually drive revenue — that is where to start

## What is SaaS Link Building?

SaaS link building is the process of earning backlinks to a software company’s website from other credible, relevant sites. These backlinks signal to search engines that your pages are trustworthy and worth ranking.

In practice, it involves editorial outreach, original research, free tools, directory placements, and brand mention reclamation — all aimed at building authority on the pages that drive pipeline. What separates saas link building from generic link building is that SaaS companies need authority on product pages and comparison pages, not just blog content, and niche relevance matters far more than raw domain rating when targeting the buyers who actually convert.

## Why SaaS Link Building Is Different

Link building for SaaS is not just link building with a SaaS logo on it. The buying cycle is longer, the buyers are more skeptical, and the pages that need authority are not always the ones that are easiest to pitch.

### The pages that actually drive revenue do not naturally attract links

Most linkable content in SaaS is educational. Blog posts, how-to guides, and research reports get linked. Pricing pages, comparison pages, and product feature pages almost never do, despite those being the pages your organic pipeline actually flows through. If you are only building saas backlinks to your blog, you are building authority in the wrong part of your site.

### Niche relevance matters more than domain rating

A DR40 SaaS operations blog linking to your project management tool is worth more than a DR80 lifestyle site linking to the same page. Google evaluates the topical relevance of the linking page, not just the authority of the domain. This is one of the most consistent things we see teams get wrong, especially when working with agencies that prioritize DR as the primary metric.

According to Ahrefs research, pages ranking in position one have 3.8 times more [backlink](/glossary/what-is-a-backlink/ "Glossary: Backlink")s than pages in positions two through ten. But volume alone does not explain the gap — relevance of those links is what separates the pages that hold their rankings under algorithm updates from the ones that drop.

## Strategy 1: Fix Which Pages You Are Building Links To

Before you pitch a single journalist or send a single outreach email, audit where your saas backlinks are currently pointing and compare that against where your pipeline actually originates.

### Find the pages with commercial value that are stuck on page two or three

These are your highest-leverage link targets. A product page, a comparison page, or a feature page sitting in position 11 to 20 is close enough to page one that a concentrated backlink push can move it. A blog post in the same position might drive traffic if it ranks, but that traffic may never convert.

Pull your [Google Search Console](/glossary/what-is-google-search-console/) data and filter for pages with high impression counts but click-through rates below 2%. Those pages are being shown to your buyers and losing the click to a competitor. Build links there first.

### Create linkable entry points that funnel authority to your product pages

No one links to a pricing page directly, but you can create a piece of content that earns links and then internally passes authority to the page that matters. A well-cited comparison post, a benchmark report, or a free tool can earn dozens of saas backlinks and route that authority exactly where you need it. This is the model HubSpot has used at scale — their most-linked content pieces consistently feed authority into their product and feature pages through tight internal linking.

## Strategy 2: Digital PR and Editorial Outreach

This is the highest-ROI saas link building strategy available to most B2B teams, and also the one most teams give up on too quickly because the feedback loop is slower than other tactics.

### Finding the pitch angle that journalists actually want

The mistake is pitching the product. Journalists do not cover SaaS tools unless they are writing a roundup. What they do cover is data, trends, expert perspectives, and stories. If you have access to usage data across your customer base, proprietary benchmarks from your product, or a founder perspective on a market shift, those are your pitch assets — not the product itself.

Drift’s Chatbots Report, which was a piece of original research they published about conversational marketing trends, earned over 350 referring domains because it gave journalists something worth citing. The product was barely mentioned.

### What gets coverage in 2026

Original research with a sharp angle, trend commentary that counters prevailing wisdom, and expert quotes that offer a perspective missing from the existing coverage. Build a media outreach list of the 30 publications your ideal buyers read, and pitch data or perspectives that are genuinely useful to their audience. An authority link building service worth hiring will already have relationships with those publications.

## Strategy 3: Original Research and Data Studies

Research attracts saas backlinks at a scale almost nothing else can match. The reason is simple: when you produce data that does not exist anywhere else, every person who wants to cite that data has to link to you.

### How to produce a data study without a dedicated research team

You do not need a research firm. Your customer base and product data are research assets you already own. Survey your customers on a topic your buyers care about. Pull usage trends from your product analytics. Compare publicly available data across [competitors](/blogs/how-to-do-saas-seo-competitor-analysis/ "SaaS SEO Competitor Analysis") in your category and synthesize it into something original. Even a modest sample of 100 to 200 responses produces enough data for a publishable report if the question is specific and the framing is sharp.

Venngage’s annual design trend report earned over 400 referring domains from a single publication cycle. That level of organic link acquisition from one asset compounds every year the report is refreshed and reshared.

### Make the data specific enough to be citable

Broad findings do not get cited. “Most SaaS companies struggle with customer retention” is too generic to link to. A specific, quantified finding with clear parameters is the kind of data that journalists, analysts, and other content creators will link to and reference. Build your research questions to produce that kind of specificity.

## Strategy 4: Free Tools and Calculators

A well-built free tool is one of the most durable link building assets a SaaS company can produce, because it solves a problem your buyer already has and gives other sites a reason to reference it permanently.

### Picking the right tool for your [ICP](/glossary/what-is-icp/)

The tool has to be genuinely useful to the exact buyer you are trying to reach. A project management SaaS could build a sprint velocity calculator. A financial operations SaaS could build a burn rate calculator or a runway estimator. A sales enablement tool could build a quota attainment tracker. The framing should be: what calculation does my buyer do manually every week that I could automate in a tool?

Clockify’s time card calculator is one of their top five most-linked pages despite being a simple utility. It earns saas backlinks from HR blogs, time management guides, and productivity roundups — all audiences with high overlap with their target buyers.

### Get the tool listed and referenced in the right places

Once your tool exists, submit it to relevant directories, list it in SaaS roundups and “free tools for [role]” compilations, and pitch it as a resource reference to any publication writing about the problem it solves. These three actions alone can produce consistent inbound links over a multi-year period from a single tool investment.

## Strategy 5: SaaS Directories, Review Sites, and Integration Ecosystems

These are the most overlooked category of saas backlinks, largely because they feel mechanical. But they are the foundation every other strategy builds on, and they are often completely free.

### The listings every SaaS company should have

G2, Capterra, Software Advice, GetApp, and Product Hunt are the obvious ones. Beyond those, look for any category-specific software directory that serves your buyers. A legal ops tool should be listed on legal technology directories. A construction project management tool should appear on construction industry software lists. These vertical directories often carry significant authority with their specific audiences and produce referral traffic alongside the backlink value.

### Integration marketplace listings as an underused link source

If your product integrates with Zapier, HubSpot, Salesforce, or any major platform, get your integration listed in their marketplace. Zapier’s integration directory alone carries substantial [domain authority](/glossary/what-is-domain-authority/), and the links are highly relevant to your product category. Most SaaS companies set up the integration and forget to claim the marketplace listing — a consistent and entirely free source of high-relevance saas backlinks.

## Strategy 6: Unlinked Brand Mention Reclamation

Every time someone writes about your product, recommends it in an article, or references your company without linking, you have a link that already exists in spirit but not in practice. Reclaiming those mentions is one of the fastest wins available in saas link building.

### How to find your unlinked mentions

Set up Google Alerts for your brand name and your most distinctive product features. Use Ahrefs Alerts if you have an account — filter for mentions without a backlink. Review your [Google Search Console](/glossary/what-is-google-search-console/) coverage data for references you can spot from traffic patterns. For most SaaS companies that have been around two or more years, this exercise surfaces 10 to 20 genuinely easy link opportunities within the first week.

### The outreach approach that actually converts

Be specific about which sentence the link should go on, provide the exact URL and [anchor text](/glossary/what-is-anchor-text/ "Glossary: Anchor Text") you want them to link to, and frame the request as a helpful correction rather than a favor. Something like: “I noticed you referenced [product] in your article about [topic] — we would love to be linked there if you are ever doing an update.” Short, no pressure, direct. Conversion rates on these outreach requests run significantly higher than cold outreach for a new placement because the editor has already demonstrated they know and like your product.

## Strategy 7: AI Visibility and the Emerging Link Building Equation

This is the part of saas link building almost no guide in 2026 covers in depth, and it is the one that will matter most over the next two to three years.

### Why AI search changes which links matter

Search engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini are citing sources differently than Google does. They pull from authoritative references, well-cited content, and brand mentions even when there is no direct hyperlink. Ahrefs research found that brand mentions are weighted roughly three times more heavily by AI engines than traditional backlinks when determining what gets surfaced in AI-generated answers.

This means a strategy focused purely on hyperlink acquisition is already incomplete. Building brand visibility in the publications, communities, and reference materials that AI engines use as training sources is now part of what a comprehensive saas link building strategy looks like.

### What this looks like in practice

Get your brand and your team’s perspectives into high-authority publications your buyers read. Pursue HARO and editorial outreach not just for the link but for the byline and the brand mention. Get listed in “best SaaS tools” roundups and category comparison posts even when those posts do not link directly to you. Be a source in industry research. Over time, the pattern of mentions across credible sources is what signals to AI engines that your product deserves to be surfaced when buyers ask for recommendations.

## How to Measure SaaS Link Building ROI

The absence of measurement is why most saas link building strategies eventually get defunded. If you cannot connect backlink acquisition to something the business cares about, link building becomes the first thing cut when budgets tighten.

This fits inside the wider [SaaS SEO 8-step framework](/blogs/saas-seo-strategy-and-framework/ "SaaS SEO Strategy & Framework").

### The metrics that actually matter

- **Keyword rank movement on your target URLs** — not across your whole site, but specifically the product and comparison pages you identified in Strategy 1
- **Organic sessions to those pages** — are more buyers reaching the pages that convert?
- **[Domain authority](/glossary/what-is-domain-authority/) or domain rating trend** — directional health check, not the primary metric
- **Pipeline influenced by organic** — what opportunities originated from a page that benefitted from your link building work?

Track these at the URL level, not the domain level. A 10-position rank improvement on your pricing page is worth far more than a 2-point domain rating increase, and it is the metric your leadership team will actually care about.

### What reasonable timelines look like

In our experience, the first signs of rank movement on target URLs show up between months two and four, depending on how competitive the keyword is. Consistent pipeline attribution to organic from your newly ranking pages typically becomes visible between months five and nine. Link building is a long investment, but when it is done with the right page targets and the right sources, it does not reset to zero the way paid spend does.

## Frequently Asked Questions About SaaS Link Building

### 1. What is SaaS link building and why is it different from regular link building?

SaaS link building is the process of acquiring backlinks specifically to support a software-as-a-service company’s organic search visibility and pipeline goals. The difference from general link building is primarily in where the links need to go and who you are trying to reach. SaaS buyers are sophisticated, do long research cycles, and evaluate multiple solutions before speaking to sales. That means the pages that need authority are not just blog posts — they are product pages, comparison pages, and feature pages that appear during a buyer’s evaluation process. Standard link building advice rarely accounts for this.

### 2. How many backlinks does a SaaS company need to rank on page one?

There is no fixed number, but the pattern we consistently observe is that the gap between page one and page two is mostly explained by relevance and domain authority of linking sites, not raw volume. According to Ahrefs data, position-one pages have 3.8 times more backlinks than positions two through ten on average. Rather than chasing a number, focus on matching the authority and relevance profile of the pages currently ranking above you for your target keywords. That is a more actionable benchmark than any fixed link count.

### 3. How long does SaaS link building take to show results?

Most teams see initial rank movement on target URLs within two to four months of consistent link acquisition. Meaningful pipeline attribution — where organic from those pages starts contributing to demo requests and closed deals — typically takes five to nine months. The timeline compresses significantly when you target pages already ranking in positions 11 to 20, because those pages need fewer incremental links to move onto page one. Starting with those quick-win targets while building out your long-term link assets is how the best programs structure the first six months.

### 4. Should I build links to my product pages or my blog?

Both, but your product and comparison pages should be the priority, especially if you are working with a limited budget or link capacity. Blog posts naturally attract links through content distribution. Product pages almost never attract links organically, which means the only way they get saas backlinks is through deliberate strategy. The blog can support your product pages through internal linking once it has accumulated authority, but treating the blog as your primary link building target while your product pages sit with no backlinks is one of the most common and costly mistakes we see in B2B [SaaS SEO](/blogs/saas-seo/ "SaaS SEO Strategy") programs.

### 5. What makes a backlink high quality for a SaaS company?

The three factors that actually matter: topical relevance of the linking page to your product category, domain authority of the linking site, and whether the link is editorial (placed within real content) rather than listed (directory-style or footer). A link from a project management blog to a project management tool, placed within an article about sprint planning, is high quality. A link from a general marketing directory pointing to the same tool is worth less than half as much, regardless of the directory’s domain rating. Relevance first, authority second, editorial placement third.

### 6. What is AI visibility link building and does it matter now?

AI visibility link building refers to the practice of acquiring brand mentions and backlinks specifically in the sources that AI search engines like Perplexity, ChatGPT, and Gemini use to form their answers. These engines cite authoritative publications, well-referenced brand names, and content that appears consistently across credible sources. Ahrefs research found that brand mentions are weighted roughly three times more heavily by AI engines than traditional backlinks. For SaaS companies targeting buyers who increasingly start their research with AI-generated answers rather than a Google search, building visibility in the right reference sources is no longer optional.

### 7. Should I hire a saas link building agency or do it in-house?

In-house link building makes sense when you have a dedicated content and [SEO](/glossary/what-is-seo/) resource with time to run consistent outreach campaigns, build editorial relationships, and manage a growing backlink tracking setup. It breaks down when you need to scale quickly, when your niche requires existing publisher relationships you do not have, or when your team’s time is better spent on other growth levers. An authority link building service is worth evaluating when the opportunity cost of in-house effort is high, when you are in a competitive niche where link quality and velocity matter, and when you want a team that already has the relationships and infrastructure to move faster than you could build from scratch.
