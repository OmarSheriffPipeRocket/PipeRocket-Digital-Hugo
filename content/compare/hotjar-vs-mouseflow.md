## Strengths & tradeoffs

Both are excellent at the core job (heatmaps plus session replay). The honest differences are at the edges, and each side wins some rows.

| Axis | Hotjar | Mouseflow |
|---|---|---|
| **Scope** | Broad suite: behavior plus surveys and recruited interviews | Focused on quantitative behavior diagnostics |
| **Setup and ease** | Famously fast, no-code, common first CRO tool | Quick to install, steeper learning curve on its standout features |
| **Funnels and forms** | Funnels in Observe; form behavior via recordings | Stronger: multi-step funnels and field-level form analytics |
| **Friction signals** | Rage-click and similar signals within recordings | Automatic per-session friction score across all sessions |
| **Voice-of-customer** | Surveys (Ask) and recruited interviews (Engage) | Feedback surveys only; no recruited interviews |
| **Pricing structure** | Three products priced separately; ~€32/mo Observe Plus (verify live) | One product, session-based tiers; $25/mo Essential |
| **Value on session volume** | Daily-cap model; cost depends on products needed | More monthly sessions per dollar |
