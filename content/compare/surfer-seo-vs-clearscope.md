## Strengths & tradeoffs

Both tools do the core job well (grade a draft against the top-ranking pages so it covers the expected ground). The honest differences are at the edges, and each side wins some rows.

| Axis | Surfer SEO | Clearscope |
|---|---|---|
| **Scope** | Research, AI writing, and live scoring in one editor | Focused NLP grader (A++ to F); one job, done consistently |
| **AI writing** | Built-in AI writer across plans | None by design |
| **Seats** | Per-seat / per-workspace tiers; cost grows with team | Unlimited users and projects on every plan |
| **Entry price** | ~$49/mo (Discovery, billed yearly) | ~$129/mo (Essentials) |
| **Full capability** | Pro at $182/mo (Discovery is a limited entry point) | Business at $399/mo; Essentials already includes unlimited users |
| **Public proof** | 4.8 on G2 across 537 reviews; G2 Leader badge | 4.9 on G2, but a smaller pool of 92 reviews |
| **Scoring evidence** | 0.28 ranking correlation documented (aiflowreview.com) | Clean, repeatable grading; vendor case study cites a 721% organic-clicks lift (treat as vendor figure) |

On raw rating, Clearscope edges ahead at 4.9/5, but that sits on 92 reviews against Surfer's 537, so Surfer's 4.8 rests on far more statistical depth. Surfer also carries an independently documented scoring-to-ranking correlation, while Clearscope's headline 721% lift is a vendor case-study figure rather than third-party verified. Weigh which form of evidence (rating height versus review depth) you trust more, and remember the choice is really about breadth versus focus, not which tool is "better."
