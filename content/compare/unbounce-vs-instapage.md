  heading: "The short answer"
  intro: >-
    Unbounce and Instapage are two of the longest-standing SaaS landing page
    platforms. Both give marketers a no-code builder, A/B testing, and
    integrations with ad and CRM stacks. The honest split comes down to scope
    and starting price: Unbounce enters at $29/mo, includes popups and sticky
    bars, and adds AI traffic optimization (Smart Traffic) at its Optimize tier.
    Instapage starts at $99/mo with native real-time team collaboration from
    the start, and adds AdMap ad-to-page alignment plus audience-level
    personalization at its Optimize tier ($199/mo); built-in heatmaps sit on
    its Convert (enterprise) plan.
  callouts:
    - label: "Choose Unbounce"
      title: "Lower entry price with popups, sticky bars, and Smart Traffic"
      body: >-
        If you are a small team or solo marketer, budget-sensitive (starting at
        **$29/mo**), running multi-client agency work, or need **popups and
        sticky bars** alongside landing pages, Unbounce is the broader, more
        affordable entry point. The **14-day trial requires no credit card**.
        Full AI optimization (Smart Traffic) kicks in at $249/mo.
    - label: "Choose Instapage"
      title: "AdMap, heatmaps, and team collaboration for paid-media teams"
      body: >-
        If you run paid media at scale and need **AdMap** to visually align each
        ad with a dedicated page, **audience-level personalization** (UTM, geo,
        device), and **real-time collaborative review** workflows, Instapage is
        the more complete CRO tool starting at **$199/mo** (Optimize plan). Note
        that **built-in heatmaps** sit on its Convert (enterprise) plan.

faqs:
  - q: "What is the difference between Unbounce and Instapage?"
    a: >-
      Unbounce is a landing page builder with popups, sticky bars, and AI Smart
      Traffic optimization, starting at $29/mo. Instapage is a post-click
      optimization platform with AdMap ad-to-page alignment, built-in heatmaps,
      audience-level personalization, and real-time team collaboration, starting
      at $99/mo. Unbounce does more for the budget-sensitive team; Instapage
      does more for the paid-media team running ads at scale.
  - q: "Is Unbounce better than Instapage?"
    a: >-
      Neither is better in all cases. Unbounce is better for teams that need
      a lower starting price, popups and sticky bars, or a no-card free trial.
      Instapage is better for paid-media teams that want AdMap, native heatmaps,
      audience-level personalization, and collaborative review workflows. The
      right pick depends on your team size, ad spend, and which specific
      features your workflow actually requires.
  - q: "Which is cheaper, Unbounce or Instapage?"
    a: >-
      Unbounce is cheaper at entry: $29/mo (Starter, monthly) versus
      Instapage's $99/mo (Create), as of June 2026. Unbounce's full-feature
      Optimize plan is $249/mo, while Instapage's equivalent Optimize plan is
      $199/mo, making Instapage closer to parity at the capable tier.
      Annual pricing lowers both; Instapage does not publish its annual
      monthly-equivalent for self-serve plans, so verify live.
  - q: "Does Instapage have popups and sticky bars like Unbounce?"
    a: >-
      No. Instapage does not offer popups or sticky bars. Those features are
      available on Unbounce from its Build plan ($99/mo) upward. If popups and
      sticky bars are a requirement, Unbounce is the right pick.
  - q: "Does Unbounce have AdMap or built-in heatmaps like Instapage?"
    a: >-
      No. Unbounce does not offer AdMap (the feature that visually links each
      ad to a dedicated landing page) and has no native heatmap tool. You would
      need a third-party tool such as Hotjar for heatmaps. Instapage includes
      AdMap on its Optimize plan ($199/mo) and heatmaps on its Convert (enterprise) plan.
  - q: "Do Unbounce and Instapage offer a free plan or free trial?"
    a: >-
      As of June 2026, neither offers a permanent free plan. Unbounce provides
      a 14-day free trial with no credit card required. Instapage's 14-day
      trial on Create and Optimize plans requires a credit card; its Convert
      (enterprise) plan requires a demo call with no trial. Verify current
      trial terms on each vendor's pricing page before starting.

## Strengths & tradeoffs

Both platforms handle the core job of no-code landing page creation and conversion testing. Each wins clearly on different axes, and neither is an across-the-board winner.

| Axis | Unbounce | Instapage |
|---|---|---|
| **Starting price** | $29/mo (Starter, monthly); lowest entry in class | $99/mo (Create); higher floor but unlimited pages from the start |
| **Free trial** | 14 days, no credit card required | 14 days, credit card required; Convert plan demo only |
| **Popups and sticky bars** | Built in from $99/mo Build plan | Not available at any plan |
| **AI optimization** | Smart Traffic on Optimize ($249/mo); claims ~30% lift (vendor figure) | AI experiments on Optimize ($199/mo) |
| **AdMap** | Not available | Optimize ($199/mo) and above; visually maps each ad to a dedicated page |
| **Heatmaps** | No native tool; requires Hotjar or similar | Built in on Convert (enterprise) plan |
| **Personalization** | DTR (keyword-level) only | UTM, geo, device-based segments on Optimize |
| **Team workflow** | Seat-count only; no native commenting or approvals | Real-time commenting, approvals, version history on all plans |
| **Pricing transparency** | Annual pricing published across all tiers | Annual discount available but monthly-equivalent not published for self-serve tiers |
| **Overage risk** | Overage penalty reported by users (30% of annual sub/mo); verify current TOS | No overage penalty model reported; Convert requires annual contract |
