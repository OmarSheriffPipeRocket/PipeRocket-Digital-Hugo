## Strengths & tradeoffs

Both are excellent at the core job — organic growth for B2B SaaS, with AI search baked in. The honest differences are at the edges, and each side wins some rows.

| Axis | SimpleTiger | Skale |
|---|---|---|
| **Public proof** | 4.9 on Clutch across 30 reviews; multiple 2024–25 Clutch awards | 4.9 on Clutch, but only 16 reviews and no documented awards |
| **Team scale** | ~16 active staff — leaner, possible capacity ceiling | 50+ specialists — more bench for larger programs |
| **Service breadth** | SEO + AI search **plus** paid search, paid social and Webflow builds | Organic-only; no paid media at all |
| **Focus** | Broad funnel; paid is secondary to organic | Pure organic specialist, revenue-tied to SQLs/MRR |
| **Certifications** | Google, HubSpot, Webflow partner certs | None documented publicly |
| **Client roster** | Gainsight, Invoca, Gelato, Segment, JotForm | Attest, Pitch, Maze, Rezi, Holded, Perkbox |
| **Pricing entry** | Lower floor (~$5K+ Clutch min) | Higher estimated floor (~$8K+) |
| **Pricing model** | % of revenue/funding — predictability friction | Custom retainer, call-only — no transparency |
| **Geography** | US-based, fully remote | London, UK; strong European SaaS base |

On documented third-party proof, SimpleTiger is ahead: 30 reviews to Skale's 16, plus platform certifications and several Clutch awards — even though Skale is the larger agency. Skale counters with scale and focus: a 50+ person organic-only team tying every activity to revenue, and a European SaaS roster (Attest, Pitch, Maze) that's hard to match. Both ratings are identical at 4.9, so weigh review depth and certifications against team size and organic focus. And note both share the same gap — neither publishes transparent, flat-fee pricing.
