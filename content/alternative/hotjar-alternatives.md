Comparing the top 7 best Hotjar alternatives in 2026 includes 1. LogRocket, 2. Microsoft Clarity, 3. Mouseflow, 4. PostHog, 5. Lucky Orange, 6. FullStory, 7. Crazy Egg.

LogRocket is for product and engineering teams that need session replay combined with JavaScript error tracking and performance monitoring; Microsoft Clarity is the free-forever pick for any traffic volume with GA4 integration; Mouseflow suits e-commerce and growth teams that want transparent, per-session pricing without sampling; PostHog is for developer-led teams wanting an open-source, self-hostable all-in-one; Lucky Orange covers small business and e-commerce teams that want every feature without tier gating; FullStory is the enterprise option with warehouse-level behavioral data pipelines; and Crazy Egg is the marketer's lightweight heatmap-plus-A/B-testing combo.

Choosing the wrong behavior analytics tool costs more than the monthly subscription fee: you get sampled data that misrepresents friction points, performance hits that slow your pages, or a pricing cliff that forces a painful migration once traffic grows. Each tool below was evaluated on session replay fidelity, heatmap capabilities, free-plan availability, pricing transparency, and G2 reviewer sentiment. Teams most often leave Hotjar for one of three reasons: page-load slowdowns after the script installs, data sampling on lower tiers, and steeply escalating costs as traffic scales.

## TL;DR

1. **LogRocket:** Best for product and engineering teams that need session replay, JS error tracking, and performance monitoring in one tool.
2. **Microsoft Clarity:** Best for any team that wants unlimited heatmaps and session recordings at zero cost with native GA4 integration.
3. **Mouseflow:** Best for e-commerce and growth teams wanting transparent per-session pricing, unlimited users, and no data sampling.
4. **PostHog:** Best for developer-led teams that want an open-source, self-hostable platform with A/B testing, feature flags, and product analytics built in.
5. **Lucky Orange:** Best for small business and e-commerce teams wanting every CRO feature (including live chat and surveys) on every plan tier.
6. **FullStory:** Best for enterprise teams needing compliance-grade data controls and behavioral data pipelines to a data warehouse.
7. **Crazy Egg:** Best for marketers and CRO practitioners who need heatmaps plus a native A/B testing and popup builder in a low-cost tool.

## How We Chose These Tools?

Each tool on this list was evaluated using G2 ratings and review commentary, supplemented by qualitative signals from CRO, product, and growth practitioner discussions on Reddit, LinkedIn, and Quora. Pricing was verified directly from live product pages as of June 2026, and any field that could not be confirmed from a primary source is flagged in the relevant card below.

The two criteria that separated the shortlist from the broader field: session replay fidelity without sampling (the most common complaint about Hotjar's lower tiers is that data is sampled once the plan allowance is exceeded, meaning you miss the sessions that matter most) and total cost of ownership at scale (Hotjar's pricing jumps steeply with traffic, so a true alternative must offer a more predictable cost curve or a free tier that doesn't cap out at a useless volume).

It's NOT for teams on a tight budget or those who only need basic heatmaps and feedback widgets. At $295/mo for the full feature set, it's steep compared with Hotjar's entry price. The free tier's 1,000 sessions/mo cap is very low for any meaningful traffic volume.

**Core Strengths**

LogRocket's differentiator is the error-tracking layer: no other tool in this set pairs session replay with frontend JS error detection as tightly. The Galileo AI feature auto-surfaces UX friction and assigns severity scores, cutting triage time for engineering and product teams.

**Known Gaps**

The free tier (1,000 sessions/mo) is impractical for real analysis. Core starts at $69/mo for a session range that may still feel tight. Teams that only need heatmaps and feedback surveys will pay for features they don't use.

- Free tier capped at 1,000 sessions/mo: very low for meaningful analysis

Best for: Teams on any budget needing unlimited heatmaps and session recordings with zero cost and native GA4 integration.

[Microsoft Clarity](https://clarity.microsoft.com) is a completely free behavior analytics tool from Microsoft that offers unlimited session recordings, unlimited heatmaps, rage-click and dead-click detection, and native GA4 integration. It earns its place here as the only tool in this set with zero cost at any traffic scale: there is no plan cap, no credit card, and no expiry.

**Tool Snapshot**

| | |
| --- | --- |
| Platform | Web app |
| Free Plan | Completely free, unlimited heatmaps and recordings, no plan cap, no credit card |
| Starting Price | Free |
| G2 Rating | [4.5/5 (55 reviews)](https://www.g2.com/products/microsoft-microsoft-clarity/reviews) |

**Fit Profile**

It's for any team, at any traffic volume, that wants the core behavioral data layer (heatmaps, session recordings, frustration signals) without paying for it. It's especially strong for teams already on GA4 who want the behavioral context alongside their analytics.

It's NOT for teams that need surveys, feedback widgets, or user interview scheduling. Clarity has no qualitative research layer, which is one of Hotjar's most-used features. There's also no A/B testing and limited advanced reporting.

**Core Strengths**

The pitch is simple: everything Hotjar charges $49+/mo for on the behavioral side (heatmaps, recordings, rage-click detection) is available in Clarity at no cost, at any scale, with automatic PII masking built in so there's no CSS selector setup.

- Completely free at any traffic volume: eliminates the cost concern entirely
- Native GA4 integration: behavioral sessions link directly to your analytics account
- Automatic PII masking and rage-click/dead-click detection built into every account

**What Users Say**

**Love:** Unlimited recordings with GA4 context
"Microsoft Clarity's unlimited sessions, superior frustration detection, and GA4 integration outweigh Hotjar's advantages for most teams." per [UX Heat comparative review](https://www.uxheat.com/blog/hotjar-vs-clarity).

- Free at any scale: reviewers consistently cite this as the primary reason they switched from Hotjar.

**Complain:** No surveys, feedback, or qualitative research tools
G2 reviewers flag that Clarity's qualitative research features are absent compared with Hotjar's survey and feedback tooling.

- Only 55 G2 reviews: a smaller review base than most tools in this set, so the rating carries less statistical weight.

**Known Gaps**

Clarity's behavioral layer is solid, but the tool stops there. No surveys, no feedback widgets, no user interviews, no A/B testing, and no advanced reporting beyond the core heatmap and session views. Teams that use Hotjar's qualitative research layer heavily will need a second tool to cover it.

- No surveys, feedback widgets, or user interview tooling
- Limited advanced reporting and no A/B testing; 55-review G2 base is small

**Our Read**

For teams that want heatmaps and session recordings without a subscription, Clarity is an obvious first stop. The GA4 integration is genuinely useful. Where it falls short is the qualitative research layer, which may push some teams to pair it with a lightweight survey tool.

Mouseflow's no-sampling commitment and unlimited users on every plan make it a more predictable operational tool than Hotjar. Friction detection (rage clicks, U-turns, error clicks) is built into every tier, not gated behind higher plans.

## Why Look for a Hotjar Alternative?

The three most common reasons teams leave Hotjar all come from the same root: the tool is being asked to do more than its original design supports at the pricing and scale involved. The first complaint is page performance. Reviewers across multiple sources flag page-load slowdowns after the Hotjar script installs, which is a direct trade-off between behavioral data and site speed. For conversion-focused teams, slower pages offset the CRO value the tool is meant to provide.

The second reason is data sampling. On lower Hotjar tiers, once the session allowance is reached, the tool samples rather than records every session. Sampled data is a problem because the sessions that don't get recorded are often the ones that matter: edge cases, low-frequency but high-value user segments, and the tail of behavior that reveals real friction. Microsoft Clarity, Mouseflow, and LogRocket all address sampling directly, either by eliminating it or by providing a no-cost unlimited tier.

The third reason is pricing at scale. Hotjar's cost grows steeply with traffic, and Contentsquare's acquisition of the platform introduces vendor lock-in risk for teams that only need the behavioral layer. Tools like PostHog's self-hosted option or Mouseflow's per-session pricing offer more predictable cost curves as traffic grows.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best Hotjar alternative?

The best Hotjar alternative depends on your use case. For teams that need session replay with JS error tracking, LogRocket is the strongest option. For free unlimited recordings, Microsoft Clarity has no peer. For open-source and self-hosting, PostHog is the only real option. For enterprise data warehouse integration, FullStory is the fit.

### Is there a free Hotjar alternative?

Yes. Microsoft Clarity is completely free at any traffic scale with unlimited heatmaps and session recordings. PostHog's cloud tier offers 5,000 recordings/mo free with no credit card. LogRocket and Mouseflow both offer free plans (1,000 and 500 sessions/mo respectively), though the caps are low.

### Which Hotjar alternative is best for e-commerce?

Mouseflow and Lucky Orange are the strongest e-commerce fits. Mouseflow offers transparent per-session pricing with no sampling and unlimited users. Lucky Orange includes live chat, surveys, funnel analytics, and heatmaps on every plan tier, which suits the multi-channel CRO workflow of e-commerce teams.

### Does any Hotjar alternative include A/B testing?

Yes. Crazy Egg includes native A/B testing and a popup/CTA builder on plans above Starter. PostHog includes A/B experiments alongside session replay and product analytics on its all-in-one platform. No other tool in this set has built-in A/B testing.

### Which Hotjar alternative is best for large enterprise teams?

FullStory is the clear enterprise pick. It offers warehouse-level behavioral data sync, AI-powered session insights (StoryAI), enterprise SSO and RBAC, and scales to millions of sessions without sampling. Note that all paid plans require a sales conversation; no public pricing is available above the free tier.

### Is Microsoft Clarity actually free?

Yes, as of June 2026, Microsoft Clarity is completely free with no plan caps, no paid tiers, and no credit card required. Unlimited heatmaps and session recordings are available to any account at any traffic volume. The trade-off is no surveys, no feedback widgets, and no A/B testing.

### What is the cheapest Hotjar alternative with real recording volume?

Microsoft Clarity is free at unlimited volume. Among paid tools, Mouseflow's Essential plan at $25/mo allows 5,000 sessions/mo with no sampling. PostHog's pay-as-you-go model starts free (5,000 recordings/mo) and bills $0.005 per session above that, making it cost-effective for low-to-moderate volumes above the free tier.
