Comparing the top 7 best Moz alternatives in 2026 includes 1. Semrush, 2. Ahrefs, 3. SE Ranking, 4. Serpstat, 5. SpyFu, 6. Mangools, 7. Ubersuggest.

Semrush is the most complete all-in-one suite for teams that want the deepest keyword and competitive database; Ahrefs is the choice for backlink depth and the largest verified link index; SE Ranking is the best-value full platform for SMBs that want rank tracking, audits, and keyword research without a premium price tag; Serpstat is the cheapest genuine all-in-one suite for budget-conscious agencies; SpyFu is the strongest option for teams with a significant PPC component who want competitor ad history at an affordable price; Mangools is the friendliest budget keyword-research toolkit for freelancers and bloggers; and Ubersuggest is a low-cost entry point for early-stage founders who want basic keyword ideas and site audits.

Picking the wrong tool wastes more than the subscription fee: it means your keyword volumes are off, your difficulty scores misjudge what you can realistically rank for, and your content roadmap is built on unreliable signals. Each tool below was evaluated on database size, keyword data accuracy, free-plan availability, and pricing transparency. The most common driver of Moz Pro churn is a combination of a smaller keyword and backlink database versus Semrush and Ahrefs, crawl limits that become painful at scale, and a lack of traffic-estimate columns in keyword results.

## TL;DR

1. **Semrush:** Best for teams that need the deepest all-in-one keyword and competitive database and can justify a premium price.
2. **Ahrefs:** Best for SEOs who prioritize backlink analysis and the largest frequently refreshed link index.
3. **SE Ranking:** Best for SMBs and solo SEOs that want a full platform (rank tracking, audits, keyword research) at the best value in this set.
4. **Serpstat:** Best for budget-conscious agencies that want multi-user collaboration and decent all-in-one data at a low price.
5. **SpyFu:** Best for teams with a strong PPC focus who want competitor keyword and ad history at an affordable monthly rate.
6. **Mangools:** Best for freelancers and bloggers who want the friendliest keyword-research toolkit at a budget entry price.
7. **Ubersuggest:** Best for early-stage founders who want a low-cost or lifetime-deal entry into keyword research and basic site audits.

## How We Chose These Tools?

Each tool on this list was evaluated using G2, Capterra, and qualitative signals from SEO practitioner discussions on Reddit, LinkedIn, and Quora. Pricing was verified directly from live product pages as of June 2026, and any field that could not be confirmed from a primary source is flagged in the relevant card below.

The two criteria that separated the shortlist from the broader field: database size and data freshness (the single most consistent Moz Pro complaint is a smaller keyword and backlink database relative to Semrush and Ahrefs, so alternatives needed to clearly address that gap or offer a meaningfully lower price), and pricing transparency at the plan level (strict crawl limits and a perceived price-to-depth mismatch drive most Moz churn, so value-at-tier matters).

### 2. Ahrefs

Best for: SEOs who prioritize backlink analysis and the largest frequently refreshed link index over a low entry price.

[Ahrefs](https://ahrefs.com/) is an SEO suite built around one of the largest live backlink indexes in the industry, alongside keyword research, rank tracking, and site audits. Backlink data is a documented weak point for Moz Pro, and Ahrefs is the strongest tool in this set on exactly that axis.

**Tool Snapshot**

| | |
| --- | --- |
| Platform | Web app |
| Free Plan | No free trial as of June 2026; limited free Webmaster Tools for verified sites (verify at ahrefs.com/pricing) |
| Starting Price | [$129/mo](https://ahrefs.com/pricing) (Lite, monthly; $103/mo annual) |
| G2 Rating | [4.5/5 (692 reviews)](https://www.g2.com/products/ahrefs/reviews) |

**Fit Profile**

It's for SEOs and link-building teams whose primary work is backlink analysis, competitor link research, and site audits where index freshness matters most. A Starter tier at $29/mo exists for lighter, limited use.

It's NOT for teams that want a no-commitment trial: Ahrefs doesn't offer a free trial as of June 2026. Credit-system limits on lower tiers also frustrate users who run a high volume of reports, and there's no PPC intelligence to speak of.

**Core Strengths**

Ahrefs' backlink index is among the largest and most frequently refreshed in any tool at this price level. Its Keywords Explorer and Site Explorer are consistently rated accurate and deep by practitioners, and Domain Rating (DR) is a widely accepted industry shorthand.

- One of the largest live backlink indexes in the industry: the standout data upgrade over Moz Pro's smaller link database
- Keywords Explorer and Site Explorer praised for traffic-estimate accuracy (a feature Moz Pro lacks)
- Domain Rating (DR) metric widely used as an industry shorthand alongside Moz's DA

**Standout feature:** The backlink index is the core reason teams pay for Ahrefs over Moz Pro. For competitor link research and link-gap analysis, it's the benchmark in this set, and traffic-volume estimates per keyword are a workflow feature Moz's Explorer doesn't include.

**What Users Say**

**Love:** Reviewers across [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/ahrefs/reviews) consistently rate Ahrefs highly for backlink and keyword data accuracy, with the link index and Site Explorer named most often as the standout.

- 4.5/5 across 692 G2 reviews: strong, consistent satisfaction on data quality.

**Complain:** "No trial and a credit-based usage model on lower tiers"
The lack of any free trial makes pre-purchase evaluation hard, and credit limits on the Lite plan frustrate heavy users.

- Entry price ($129/mo Lite) is noticeably higher than Moz Pro's Standard plan.

**Known Gaps**

No free trial as of June 2026 is a real evaluation hurdle. The Lite plan's credit limits cap how many reports you can run per month, and the Starter tier is very restricted in feature scope. For all-in-one breadth at a comparable price, SE Ranking is a closer fit.

- No free trial (verify live before purchase)
- Credit and usage limits on lower tiers; Starter excludes most research features

**Our Read**

Ahrefs is the right call when backlinks are the bottleneck. Moz Pro's link database is one of its most cited weaknesses; Ahrefs is the most direct fix for that specific gap.

## Why Look for a Moz Pro Alternative?

The most common reason teams leave Moz Pro is a database ceiling that becomes obvious once they start doing serious competitive keyword research or backlink analysis. Moz's keyword database and backlink index are smaller than Semrush's and Ahrefs' by a wide margin, and that gap compounds as teams get more sophisticated: you can only find the opportunities your tool surfaces, and if the database is smaller, you're working with a subset of the real picture. Reviewers on G2 and community discussions on Reddit and SEO-focused Slack groups consistently point to this as the top reason for switching.

The second driver is crawl limits. Moz Pro's crawl caps become painful for sites above 200,000 pages, which is a common threshold for SaaS products with large documentation libraries, e-commerce catalogs, or blog archives. Semrush and Ahrefs handle larger sites without hitting the same walls. SE Ranking and Serpstat are also more generous on project and crawl limits at equivalent price points.

The third reason is missing features: no traffic-estimate column in keyword results, weak content optimization compared with tools like Surfer or Clearscope (which aren't in this set but compete on that axis), and no PPC intelligence. SpyFu, Semrush, and even Ubersuggest address parts of that gap, depending on what your team needs most.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best Moz Pro alternative?

Semrush is the strongest all-round Moz Pro alternative in 2026 for teams that need a larger keyword and competitive database. Ahrefs is the best replacement if backlink data is the primary gap. SE Ranking is the best-value alternative for teams that want a full platform at a lower price.

### Is Semrush better than Moz Pro?

For most use cases, yes. Semrush has a larger keyword database, broader competitive intelligence tools including PPC ad history, and a higher G2 rating. The trade-off is price: Semrush Pro starts at $139.95/mo versus Moz's $49/mo Starter tier. If data depth is the bottleneck, the upgrade is worth it.

### Is Ahrefs better than Moz Pro?

Ahrefs leads Moz Pro on backlink index size, index freshness, and traffic-estimate accuracy in keyword results. Moz Pro has a lower entry price and a gentler learning curve. For teams whose primary need is backlink research or competitor link analysis, Ahrefs is the clearer choice.

### What is the cheapest Moz Pro alternative?

Mangools is the cheapest with an annual Entry plan at $19.90/mo. Ubersuggest is the cheapest month-to-month at a reported $29/mo Individual (verify live). Among full all-in-one suites, Serpstat at $50/mo Individual is the lowest-cost option with keyword research, rank tracking, audits, and backlinks in one plan.

### Does Semrush offer a free trial?

Yes. As of June 2026, Semrush offers a 7-day free trial, but it requires a credit card to activate. SE Ranking is the only tool in this set with a no-credit-card 14-day trial; Mangools offers a 10-day no-card trial plus a limited permanent free plan.

### Which Moz alternative has the best backlink data?

Ahrefs runs one of the largest and most frequently refreshed backlink indexes in the industry and is the benchmark in this set specifically for link data. Semrush is a close second. Both are significantly ahead of Moz Pro, Serpstat, SpyFu, and Mangools on this axis.

### Is SE Ranking a good Moz Pro replacement?

For most SMBs and small agencies, yes. SE Ranking offers a full platform at a price below Moz Pro's Standard tier, with the highest G2 satisfaction score in this set (4.8/5 across 1,432 reviews) and a no-credit-card 14-day trial. Its databases are smaller than Semrush's and Ahrefs', but for typical SMB competitive research, the gap is manageable.
