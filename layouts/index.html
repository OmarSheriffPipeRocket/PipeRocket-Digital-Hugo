How your company grows
shapes how we work

PLG and SLG aren't just labels. They represent fundamentally different marketing problems. The push to the strategy around your motion, not a standard playbook.

Product Led Growth (PLG)

Product-led growth has a specific marketing problem. The product sells itself once someone tries it. The gap is getting the right buyers into the trial in the first place.

We have built SEO, content, and paid systems specifically for PLG companies, designed to capture buyers already evaluating solutions and get them into your product before a competitor does.

Sales Led Growth (SLG)

Sales-led growth has a different problem. The sales team can close. What they need is a pipeline worth working.

We have built paid and organic systems specifically for SLG companies, designed around real ICP and buying triggers so what reaches sales is qualified, fits the profile, and is worth picking up the phone for.

Working With PipeRocket

The problem you face
determines how we start.

Every SaaS startup has different marketing problems. We start by asking which one is yours, then build a system around it, not the other way around.

Building the engine.

Most early-stage SaaS companies go through the same cycle. Launch paid campaigns, publish SEO content, and the pipeline does not move. So the strategy changes, the agency gets switched, the budget gets cut. Then it starts again. The root cause in almost always the same: the ICP was not clear enough before the engine was built.

We fix that before touching a single channel. Get the ICP tight first, then build paid and organic around your real buyers re-discovering with attribution in place from day one so you know what is actually working before you put more money behind it.

Scaling what works.

What got you here will not get the buyers you need. Moving past your initial cohort gets harder. Channels that worked stop working, take longer to convert, and reality fills out before traffic and the numbers stop making sense.

We rebuild for where you are going. ABM for accounts that need pipeline-building from scratch. Content written for buying committees, not just the one person who clicked. Paid and SEO rebuild around the new ICP with attribution that accounts for longer sales cycles and more decision-makers in the room.

Frequently asked questions

What is a SaaS SEO agency? +
A SaaS SEO agency specializes in helping SaaS companies grow their business through organic search. They combine technical SEO expertise with an understanding of the SaaS customer journey to attract qualified leads, increase website traffic, and ultimately drive conversions.
What is a SaaS pipeline? +
A SaaS pipeline is the series of stages a potential customer moves through, from awareness to closed-won deal — usually tracked in a CRM and worked by sales.
What is organic growth in SaaS? +
Growth driven by non-paid channels — organic search, content, referrals, community — where the channel cost is fixed but reach compounds over time.
Why is SEO important for B2B SaaS? +
B2B SaaS buyers research independently before talking to sales. If you're not visible in organic search when they're researching, you're not in their consideration set when they're ready to buy.
Which technical SEO factors are crucial for B2B SaaS websites? +
Site speed, indexability, schema markup for software/product/FAQ, internal linking that surfaces buying-stage pages, and Core Web Vitals — in roughly that order of impact.
