Why cybersecurity marketing is different

Generic SaaS playbooks die in front of a CISO who’s seen ten thousand vendor decks and trusts none of them. Here’s what changes when your buyer’s job is to spot vendor BS.

CISOs are trained to spot hype

Every adjective without proof is a credibility loss. "Best-in-class", "AI-powered", "enterprise-grade" — CISOs filter these out instantly. Marketing has to lead with verifiable evidence: detection rates, false-positive numbers, dwell-time data, third-party tests.

Compliance gates procurement

SOC 2, ISO 27001, FedRAMP, GDPR, HIPAA, PCI — every certification is a buying criterion. If your platform isn’t in the certification table on page one, the deal dies before security review begins.

Analyst placements drive trust

Gartner Magic Quadrant, Forrester Wave, GigaOm Radar, IDC MarketScape — CISOs use them as buying shortlists. Marketing that ignores analyst positioning loses to competitors that synchronise messaging with analyst language.

Threat-model-aware messaging

A CISO at a bank has a different threat model than a CISO at a healthtech startup or a federal agency. Generic "protection" copy wastes the buyer’s time. Programmes that speak to the specific threat model of each persona convert.
